A Rich History

About where the history of coffee begins

The story of coffee begins centuries ago in the highlands of Ethiopia, where legend has it that a goat herder named Kaldi discovered the energizing effects of coffee beans after noticing his goats became unusually lively after eating them. From these humble origins, coffee cultivation spread across the Arabian Peninsula, eventually reaching Europe and the rest of the world.

Coffee Around the World: A Global Journey of Flavors

Coffee is not just a beverage; it's a cultural phenomenon that varies vastly from one corner of the globe to another. Join us on a flavorful journey as we explore how coffee is enjoyed and celebrated around the world.
1. Ethiopia: Birthplace of Coffee
In Ethiopia, coffee isn't just a drink—it's a way of life. The country's rich coffee culture dates back centuries, with traditional coffee ceremonies playing a central role in Ethiopian social gatherings. Discover the unique flavors of Ethiopian coffee, known for its bright acidity, floral aroma, and fruity undertones.
2. Italy: Espresso and the Art of Dolce Vita
Step into a bustling Italian café, and you'll be greeted by the aromatic scent of freshly brewed espresso. Italy is renowned for its espresso culture, where coffee is served strong, short, and with a dollop of crema on top. From the iconic espresso shots enjoyed at the bar to the leisurely cappuccinos sipped in piazzas, coffee is an integral part of Italian daily life.
